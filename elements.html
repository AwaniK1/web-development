Key Elements:


1.Staple Foods: The staple food of Tibetans is tsampa, a roasted barley flour that is often mixed with
butter tea (suja) to form a dough-like consistency. This serves as a basic diet for many Tibetans,
especially nomads.


2.Meat Dishes: Due to the cold climate and high altitude, meat plays a significant role in Tibetan
cuisine. Yak meat is particularly popular, used in various forms such as yak butter, dried yak meat
(drokpa), and even yak yogurt.


3.Dairy Products: Dairy products are essential in Tibetan cuisine, with butter tea (made from yak
butter and tea leaves) being a staple drink. Yogurt, cheese (chura loen), and various milk-based sweets are also common.
