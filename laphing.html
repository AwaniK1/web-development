Laphing


Laphing is a cold, spicy, and gelatinous dish made primarily from mung bean flour or
starch, mixed with water and then set into a jelly-like texture. It is typically sliced into
strips or cubes and served cold with a variety of flavorful sauces and toppings. The dish is
known for its refreshing and tangy taste, making it a perfect snack or light meal, especially
during hot weather.

Recipe for Laphing:
Ingredients:
For the Laphing jelly: 1 cup mung bean starch
4 cups water
For the sauce (Chili oil mixture):
4 tbsp vegetable oil
4 cloves garlic, minced
1 inch ginger, minced
2 tbsp soy sauce
2 tbsp chili paste (adjust to taste)
1 tbsp vinegar
Salt to taste
Optional toppings:
Sliced cucumber
Chopped cilantro
Roasted peanuts
Sesame seeds
Instructions:
Prepare the Laphing jelly:
In a large bowl, mix the mung bean starch with water until smooth and well combined.
Pour the mixture into a large pot and bring it to a boil over medium heat, stirring constantly.
Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat to low and continue stirring until the mixture thickens to a gel-like consistency (similar to a very thick pudding).
Remove from heat and pour the mixture into a square or rectangular dish to set. Let it cool to room temperature and then refrigerate until completely set, preferably overnight.
Prepare the sauce:
Heat vegetable oil in a small pan over medium heat.
Add minced garlic and ginger, saut√© until fragrant and lightly golden.
Stir in soy sauce, chili paste, vinegar, and salt. Cook for another minute, then remove from heat and let it cool.
Assemble the Laphing:
Once the Laphing jelly has set, remove it from the refrigerator and cut it into thin strips or cubes.
Arrange the Laphing on serving plates or bowls.
Drizzle the prepared sauce generously over the Laphing.
Garnish with optional toppings like sliced cucumber, chopped cilantro, roasted peanuts, and sesame seeds.
Serve and enjoy:
Laphing is best enjoyed cold, making it a refreshing dish on hot days. The combination of spicy chili oil sauce with the cool, jelly-like texture of the Laphing creates a delightful culinary experience.
