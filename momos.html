Tibetan Momos


Tibetan momos are delightful dumplings that hold a special place in Tibetan cuisine.
These dumplings are not only delicious but also reflect the rich culinary heritage of Tibet.
Typically, momos consist of a flavorful filling enclosed in a thin dough wrapper, which is then
either steamed or fried to perfection.



Here’s a traditional recipe to make Tibetan momos:
Ingredients:
For the dough:
2 cups all-purpose flour
Water, as needed
1/2 teaspoon salt
For the filling:
1/2 lb ground meat (beef, lamb, or chicken)
1 small onion, finely chopped
2-3 cloves garlic, minced
1-inch piece of ginger, minced
2 tablespoons cooking oil
1 teaspoon ground cumin
1 teaspoon ground coriander
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
Salt and pepper, to taste
Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (optional)
Instructions:
1. Prepare the dough:
In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead until a smooth dough forms. The dough should be soft and pliable. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
2. Make the filling:
Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
Add minced garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute until fragrant.
Add the ground meat and cook until browned, breaking up any clumps with a spoon.
Stir in the cumin, coriander, turmeric powder, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few more minutes until the flavors meld together.
Remove from heat and let the filling cool completely. Stir in chopped cilantro if desired.
3. Assemble the momos:
Divide the rested dough into small walnut-sized balls. Roll each ball into a thin circle (about 3-4 inches in diameter) on a lightly floured surface. Place a spoonful of the cooled filling in the center of each circle.
To fold the momos, bring the edges together in the center, pleating them as you go, to form a pouch. Pinch the top to seal the momo. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
4. Steam the momos:
Prepare a steamer by lining it with parchment paper or lightly greased cabbage leaves.
Arrange the momos in the steamer, leaving space between each so they don’t stick together.
Steam the momos for about 15-20 minutes, or until the dough becomes translucent and the filling is cooked through.
5. Serve:
Serve hot momos immediately with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce mixed with a little vinegar or chili sauce.
