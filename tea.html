Butter Tea
Recipe for Butter Tea
Ingredients:

2 cups water
2 tablespoons loose leaf black tea (you can also use green tea if preferred)
2 tablespoons unsalted butter (traditionally yak butter is used, but you can use cow's butter)
1/4 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)

Instructions:

Boil the Water:
In a saucepan, bring 2 cups of water to a boil.

Steep the Tea:
Add 2 tablespoons of loose leaf black tea (or green tea) to the boiling water.
Let it simmer for about 5 minutes. The longer you simmer, the stronger the tea flavor will be.

Prepare the Butter:
While the tea is simmering, in a separate bowl, soften 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter to room temperature.

Mix the Ingredients:
After simmering, strain the tea into a large bowl or teapot to remove the leaves.
Pour the brewed tea into a blender or use a traditional churn (like a Tibetan churn) if available.
Add the softened butter and salt to the tea.

Blend or Churn:
Blend the mixture in a blender for about 1 minute until it becomes frothy and well-mixed. If using a churn, churn vigorously until frothy.

Serve:

Pour the butter tea into cups or bowls and serve hot.
