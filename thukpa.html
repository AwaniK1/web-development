Thukpa


Thukpa is a traditional Tibetan noodle soup that has spread across the Himalayan
region and is particularly popular in Tibet, Bhutan, Nepal, and parts of northern India.
It's a comforting and hearty dish, perfect for colder climates or as a nourishing meal any
time of the year.



Thukpa Recipe
Ingredients:
200 grams of noodles (traditionally wheat noodles or you can use rice noodles)
1 onion, thinly sliced
2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
1-inch piece of ginger, minced
1 carrot, julienned
1 bell pepper, thinly sliced (optional)
1 cup of cabbage, thinly sliced
1 cup of spinach or other leafy greens, chopped
1 tomato, diced
1 tablespoon soy sauce
1 tablespoon vegetable oil
Salt and pepper to taste
Fresh cilantro or green onions for garnish
Squeeze of lime or lemon (optional)
Instructions:
Prepare the Noodles:
Cook the noodles according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
Prepare the Soup Base:
Heat vegetable oil in a large pot or deep pan over medium heat.
Add minced garlic and ginger. Sauté for about a minute until fragrant.
Add Vegetables:
Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
Add julienned carrots and bell peppers (if using). Cook for another 2-3 minutes until they begin to soften.
Simmer:
Pour in 4-5 cups of water or vegetable broth to the pot.
Stir in diced tomatoes, cabbage, and soy sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
Season and Finish:
Season the soup with salt and pepper according to your taste.
Add the cooked noodles to the soup and stir well to combine. Let it simmer for another 2-3 minutes to heat the noodles through.
Serve:
Ladle the hot thukpa into bowls. Garnish with fresh cilantro or green onions.
Optionally, add a squeeze of lime or lemon juice for extra freshness.
