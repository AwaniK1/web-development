Tsampa


Tsampa is a fundamental staple in Tibetan cuisine, integral not only as a food source
but also culturally significant in Tibetan daily life and religious ceremonies. It's made from
roasted barley flour and is versatile, used to make porridge, dough, and even as a base for butter tea.



Recipe for Tsampa Porridge:
Ingredients:
1 cup roasted barley flour (tsampa)
1 cup boiling water
Pinch of salt (optional)
Sweeteners such as sugar, honey, or molasses (optional)
Butter or ghee (optional)
Instructions:

Prepare the Tsampa:
Place the roasted barley flour (tsampa) in a bowl.
If you prefer, you can sift the tsampa to ensure it's fine and without any lumps.

Mix with Boiling Water:
Slowly pour the boiling water over the tsampa, starting with about half a cup.
Use a spoon or chopsticks to mix the tsampa and water thoroughly. It should start to form into a crumbly texture.

Adjust Consistency:
Continue adding more boiling water, a little at a time, and mixing until you reach your desired consistency. Some people prefer a thicker porridge, while others like it more liquidy.

Add Flavorings (Optional):
If desired, add a pinch of salt to enhance the flavor.
Sweeten your tsampa porridge with sugar, honey, or molasses to taste.

Serve:
Tsampa porridge is traditionally eaten warm. You can serve it plain or topped with a knob of butter or ghee for added richness and flavor.
